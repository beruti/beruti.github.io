About

In 2014 I graduated from Newcastle University with a 2:1 degree in Economics and Business Management.

I was fascinated by Financial and Behavioural Economics but I left University wanting to build pragmatic skills and broader life experience.
As a consequence of this I worked and travelled extensively in Australia and India. Working in a solar energy company in Bangalore with Economists and Engineers I found myself drawn to the latter. I soon decided I wanted to create things and have a career that would use my social skills but be defined by a technical ability.

This is what led me to the General Assembly course and I am confident it was the right decision.
I love the problem solving aspect and build process of development and continue to marvel at the infinite angles of approach when finding a solution.

I now look forward to my first job in the Industry and to working in a challenging team environment.
